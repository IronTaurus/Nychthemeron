Basics

Realms Of Etharia is a turn based card roleplaying game, the game is sort of a mix between DnD and Magic the Gathering. The game does not have any dice making it less luck based while still retaining some random elements, a calculable randomness so to speak. The way this is that most random elements come in the form of a deck called Action Cards, these cards determine your overall damage value as well as your success rate, much like a dice does in DnD but since the deck has a limited amount of cards you can keep track on how many good or bad cards there are left in the deck. The game features a lot of customizability both by multi classing, adding removing Action Cards in your deck as well as the choice of races.

Battle System

Each player have a collection of cards representing their abilities and spells they can use. Each card is two sided with one "Sun" and one "Moon" side, whenever a player use a ability on a card they flip the card to the other side (from sun side to moon side and vice versa). Each card also have one or more types depending on what type of action the player need to use the card.

Turns & Rounds

The combat is broken down into rounds and turns, During every round each player will take a turn to do their actions.

Each round every player have 3 actions they can take during a round, one of each type:
Standard - Can only be taken during your turn.
Movement - Can only be taken during your turn.
Reaction - Can be taken anytime during the round.

Some cards can be both Standard and Reaction, these types of cards can have two function in which the player choses and use one of the two in which the time of use is determined by which ability of the card you use.
Other cards can have both types but only one function in which both Actions are used for a single card, in these cases if the card is both Reaction and Standard when the card can be used is mentioned in the ruling text of the card.

Initative

Each character draws 3 Action Cards and adds any bonuses to determine your starting initiative. The initiative is checked at the start of each Battle Round meaning that the value and turn order can be changed mid combat.

Armor

Armor will block incoming physical damage up to the value each round. Every new round resets the value. Conditions

Conditions is another big part of the battle system, a card is added to your character whenever you are inflicted by a condition. Each condition has an effect and duration all stated on the card.

Physical and Magical damage

An attack can be physical and/or magical, usually this is determined by the nature of the attack such as Melee and ranged weapons are physical while spells are magical. There are also additional types such as fire, radiant, necrotic damage. While usually the type of damage does not matter much and taking 5 points of physical damage is the same as 5 points of magic there are some cases where a character is resistant to one and not the other.

Out of Combat

Each new day resets the player's SP and also turns each card over to its sun side. Using cards out of combat works the same way as using them in combat however since Recover normally has no way to restore itself back to its sun side you need to be wary of how you use your abilities in order to not run dry when a battle starts.

Ability Check

Whenever a player makes an ability check, he or she draws 5 cards from the ActionCards deck, each card is either a Sun or a Moon, whether the sun or moon card is considered a success or fail is based on the current time being night or day.

Spending Points

A player may also spend points based on the respective stat to draw additional cards. For example if a player makes a perception (Mind) check and has a Mind stat of 3, he/she may choose to spend up to 3 points of Mind to draw that many additional cards and add it to the 3 other cards to increase the chances of succeeding. Spending these points may be done after seeing the first 3 cards and is replenished after a long rest.

Difficulty Rating

The DM determines the difficulty of a certain challenge and the player need to draw a number of success cards corresponding to that difficulty. For example: if the difficulty is 2 then the player needs 2 success amongst the card he/she draws.
