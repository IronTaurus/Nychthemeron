Basics

Nychthemeron is a turn based card roleplaying game, the game is sort of a mix between DnD and Magic the Gathering. The game does not have any dice making it less luck based while still retaining some random elements, a calculable randomness so to speak. The way this work is that most random elements come in the form of a deck called Action Cards. These cards determine your overall damage value as well as your success rate in checks, much like a dice does in DnD but since the deck has a limited amount of cards you can keep track on how many good or bad cards there are left in the deck. The game will feature a lot of customizability both by multi classing, adding/removing Action Cards in your deck as well as various different races with unique abilities.

Battle System

Each player have a collection of cards representing their abilities and spells they can use. Each card is two sided with one "Sun" and one "Moon" side, whenever a player use a ability on a card they flip the card to the other side (from sun side to moon side and vice versa). Each card also have one or more types depending on what type of action the player need to use the card.

Turns & Rounds

The combat is broken down into rounds and turns, During every round each player will take a turn to do their actions.

Each round every player have 3 actions they can take during a round, one of each type:
Standard - Can only be taken during your turn.
Movement - Can only be taken during your turn.
Reaction - Can be taken at any Moment during the round.

Some cards can be both Standard and Reaction, these types of cards can have two function and each action is used for the respective function of the card.
Other cards can have both types but only one function in which both Actions can be used for the same single function, in these cases if the card is both Reaction and Standard when and how you use the card is specified in the ruling text of the card.

Layout

1. Title of the skill/ability

2. The card type, this often determines what action is required to use the skill/ability. The different types are: Standard, Movement, Reaction, Out-of-combat, Passive and Condition.

3. Determines the face of the card, it is either Sun or Moon.

4. The Spirit cost of the card, this cost can be 0.

5. The ruletext of the card, this explain what the card does.

6. The type of attack and range modifier.
If the modifier is + or - the total value is the base range of the respective weapon equipped +/- the modifier value.
If the modifier is neither + or -, the range is set at the number indicated regardless of the base range of the weapon.

7. Requirements, this condition must be forfilled to use the card.

8. The name of the class the card belongs to.

9. The name of the sub-class the card belongs to and the tier of the card.

Active Cards

Active cards are the side of the card that is currently face up. Casting a spell does not make the other side of the card 'Active' until after the inital effect of the casted spell has been concluded and the card has been flipped over to the other side (This does not include additional after-effects such as conditions, lasting buffs and debuffs).

Initative

Each character draws 3 Action Cards and adds any bonuses to determine your starting initiative. The initiative is checked at the start of each Battle Round and the turn order is adjusted for any changes in iniative meaning that the value and turn order can be changed mid combat.

Moment and Reaction

Some cards such as those of the type Reaction can be activated on another creature’s turn at certain “Moment”s. These moments are usually printed on the card itself but in other cases such as “Patience” the Moment Condition is: ‘At any Moment’, in such cases you can refer to this table.

List of Moments:

Start

Start of a Turn or Round. (i.e. “At the start of your turn”)

Activation

Activation of a card. (i.e. “Whenever a creature makes a movement action”.)

Pre-Resolution

After the effects of a card have been determined and a resolution is imminent. (i.e. “Whenever a creature is attacked”.)

Resolution

After the resolution of a card. (i.e. “Whenever you are dealt damage”.)

End

End of a turn or round. (i.e. “At the end of the round”.)

Armor

Armor will block incoming physical damage up to its value each round. Every new round resets the value. The rules of the armor state the following: “Whenever you are dealt physical damage, for each point of damage remove an equal number of armor points and reduce damage taken equal to the number of points removed this way.” Armor points are reset at the beginning of the next round. Temporary points are always used up before the actual ones, this is the same for Temporary Health as well.

Conditions

Conditions is another big part of the battle system, a card is added to your character whenever you are inflicted by a condition. Each condition has an effect and duration all stated on the card.

Damage Types

An attack can be physical and/or magical, usually this is determined by the nature of the attack such as Melee and ranged weapons are physical while spells are magical. There are also additional types such as fire, radiant, necrotic damage. While usually the type of damage does not matter much and taking 5 points of physical damage is the same as 5 points of magic but there are some cases where a character is resistant to one and not the other.

Out of Combat

Each new day resets the player's SP and also turns each card over to its sun side. You can use certain Out of Combat cards without bigger issues but using any other card might result in you starting a combat the moment you use it.

Ability Check

Whenever a player makes an ability check, he or she draws 3 cards from the ActionCards deck, each card is either a Sun or a Moon, you add the numbers together but depending on the current Day Time the corresponding card has its value doubled. On the top of the card you can see the value it has when calculating for checks.

Difficulty Rating

The DM determines the difficulty of a certain challenge and the player need to draw cards adding their value up to or higher than that difficulty. A Player also have their own Difficulty Rating that normally starts at 6, this is what enemies need to beat in order to avoid your inflictions. This can be increased by Talents.

Attributes & Talents

A player will have a certain amount of Attribute Points in any of the 5 Attributes: Agility, Body, Mind, Mystic, Presence. Each point in an attribute also gives you an equal amount of Aspiration Points in that coresponding Attribute.
You need a certain amount of Attributes to be able to buy Talents in that category. When you level up you gain 1 attribute to put anywhere you want and 10 Talent Points to buy any Talent you wish as long as you forfill its conditions.

Aspiration Points

A player may also spend Aspiration points based on the respective stat to draw additional cards. For example if a player makes a perception (Mind) check and has a Mind Aspiration of 3, they may choose to spend up to 3 points of that stat to draw that many additional cards to increase the chances of succeeding. Spending these points may be done after seeing the first 3 cards and is replenished after a long rest.
