Eternal Gods

Eternal gods are something whose existence and power is undeniable, they don’t require prayer to enforce their power or existence and very rarely answers any. An example of an eternal god is Soln and Lunre, which are the sun and moon in Mihridahl. The eternal gods maintain the reality and natural order, they also maintain the barrier that exists between the realms so that one realm would not affect the other too much. Because of the way the eternal gods work it is often presumed that they are unaware of the existence of other gods or creatures and simply do their work.

Herald

A herald is a being chosen by the circumstances to be an aiding hand for the Ancient gods. They maintain the barrier of the realm and they are often drawn to a specific element based on the ancient god they aid.


It is not clear if Throkks father, Rovakk, was an actual herald or something else but he did have an ability that could be part of an ancient god of Naerini. His ability to bend earth to his will could be proof of that. An orc by the name of Kovash was proclaimed to be the herald of fire and maintains the barrier between Mihridahl and Cha’rhin he is a herald of the red god and can control fire.

What power being a herald may give is not known but it is usually connected to the realm of that particular god. There may be more than one herald of any one god and it does not seem to matter which realm that herald exists in as long as they are close enough to the barrier to maintain it.


There have been legends of a herald using more than one power from a single god, such as Me’teth granting both Darkness and Ice as powers to a single Herald. Normally a Herald never comes in contact with an ancient god directly, they are granted powers and a calling, you don’t hear a voice but rather feel drawn to the weak spots and can see a damaged barrier as a strange shimmer. While the barrier would be explained as a wall of sorts, it is not a clear flat wall and can take different shapes and sometimes even be partial. It is as if the barrier has no definite start or end but rather can exist anywhere and everywhere at the same time. Some have mentioned dreams where they have seen the gods partly but never have been able to interact with them.

Immortal Gods

Immortal gods are no physical creatures, instead they are ideas and beliefs manifested in Athernis to the point where it has taken the shape of a god. While they are ideas and beliefs manifested they do have a will and that is mainly to spread the idea and belief that created them to the point where everyone is a copy of themself. The power of an immortal god comes solely from their worshippers and cannot be killed by physical means. Immortal gods can under normal circumstances not exist outside Athernis.

Mortal Gods

A mortal god is a mortal being that has reached godhood, this is when they are so powerful that their power rivals the gods and become worshiped. While worshippers can grant them some power in reality most of a mortal god's power is coming from themselves. A mortal god still has a physical form and can be killed by physical means.
